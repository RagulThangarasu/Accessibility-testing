WCAG Compliance Levels Explained

Level A

Minimum

The most basic web accessibility features. Without Level A conformance, some users will find it impossible to access your content.

  • Non-text content has text alternatives
  • Captions for prerecorded audio
  • Info and relationships conveyed through presentation can be programmatically determined
  • Use of color is not the only visual means of conveying information
  • All functionality available from a keyboard

Level AA

Recommended

Deals with the biggest barriers for disabled users. This is the level most regulations require and is recommended for most websites.

  • Captions for live audio
  • Audio descriptions for prerecorded video
  • Minimum contrast ratio of 4.5:1 for text
  • Text can be resized up to 200%
  • Images of text are avoided where possible
  • Multiple ways to navigate the site
  • Headings and labels are descriptive
  • Focus is visible
  • Language of page parts can be determined
  • Consistent navigation and identification
  • Error prevention for legal/financial submissions

Level AAA

Enhanced

The highest level of accessibility. Not required for entire sites, but recommended for specific content where possible.

  • Sign language for prerecorded audio
  • Extended audio descriptions
  • Enhanced contrast ratio of 7:1
  • No background audio or can be turned off
  • Visual presentation has specific requirements
  • Reading level considerations
  • Pronunciation information available
  • Interruptions can be postponed
  • Re-authentication maintains data
  • Help is available in multiple ways

About This Tool

This accessibility scanner uses axe-core, the world's most trusted accessibility testing engine, to identify WCAG violations across your website.

Features

  • Full Site Crawling: Automatically discovers and scans all pages on your site
  • WCAG 2.0/2.1 Testing: Tests against Level A, AA, and AAA criteria
  • Detailed Reports: PDF and JSON reports with actionable recommendations
  • Impact Analysis: Issues categorized by severity (Critical, Serious, Moderate, Minor)

How to Use

  1. Enter the URL of the website you want to scan
  2. Select the WCAG compliance level (AA is recommended)
  3. Set the maximum number of pages to scan
  4. Click "Start Scan" and wait for the results
  5. Review the issues and download the PDF report

Limitations

Automated testing can detect approximately 30-40% of accessibility issues. Manual testing with assistive technologies is recommended for comprehensive accessibility audits.

Compliance Frameworks

This tool helps you meet requirements for:

  • WCAG 2.0 / WCAG 2.1 Web Content Accessibility Guidelines
  • Section 508 (US Federal agencies)
  • ADA (Americans with Disabilities Act)
  • EN 301 549 (European accessibility standard)
